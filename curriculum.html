  • Why a committee beats a single model (deliberation, checks, roles)
  • Local-first vs cloud: own your stack, own your costs, own your data

Module 2

Your Local Stack (Consumer Hardware, $0 in API Bills)

Stand up free/local models on the PC you already own.

  • Installing Ollama; choosing models for your hardware (3B → 32B; the speed/quality tradeoff)
  • Embeddings locally (nomic-embed-text / MiniLM) — why you need them
  • The "premium tokens for reasoning, free models for grunt work" routing rule
  • A tiny backend to tie it together (FastAPI pattern) — keep it lean

Module 3

The Memory Backbone (So Your AI Compounds)

Without memory you have a goldfish. With it, you have an asset that grows.

  • Episodic vs semantic memory, in plain English
  • RAG without the buzzwords: embed → store → recall
  • Standing up a real memory engine locally (the mem0 pattern; vector store under your data dir)
  • Feeding it your own history so every session remembers past work

Artifact: a drop-in episodic-memory add/search module (free/local config).

Capabilities, tools, and the pattern that lets your org grow new abilities.

  • Tools & MCP: connecting your AI to the real world (files, web, APIs) safely
  • Permissions & guardrails — sensitive-action gating before anything risky runs
  • Wiring a new skill end-to-end (worked example)

Module 5

The Trading Desk (Paper-First, Human-Gated)

A multi-agent committee that deliberates a thesis and manages a paper portfolio. No real money, ever, without you.

  • The committee: researcher, risk, bull/bear, portfolio manager — roles that argue to a decision
  • Signals worth watching (congressional-trades disclosures, whales) — APIs, not magic
  • Paper-trading & backtesting: prove it before you ever risk a dollar
  • The go-live gate: how to wire a human-press-to-trade boundary (and why you must)
  • Risk controls: position sizing, stops, the rules that keep you solvent

Artifact: the paper-committee scaffold + the human-approval gate template. In-lesson banner: not financial advice; educational; paper-first; trading risks loss.

Module 6

The Content & Business Engine (Your Audience Flywheel)

One funnel: research → script → produce → clip → publish → monetize.

  • The ONE-funnel insight: audience is the compounding asset that feeds every income stream
  • The content pipeline (research→script→produce→clip) with local models doing the grunt work
  • Monetization plug-ins: digital products, ads, affiliates — wired to the same audience
  • The publish gate: AI drafts, you approve, then it goes out (never auto-publish)

Artifact: the content-engine workflow map + a product/sales-page starter (this course is the example).

Module 7

The Self-Healing Back-Office (It Runs Itself)

The departments that keep the machine alive while you sleep: MD, IT, Security.

  • The MD (health): watchdogs that detect + self-triage (stalls, failures)
  • The IT dept: self-rebuild/upgrade; discovering better tools automatically
  • The Security/Compliance dept: red-teaming yourself; secrets hygiene; the money/publish guardrails
  • An hourly "architect beat": one loop that scans, fixes the fixable, and stays quiet otherwise

Artifact: a scanner + watchdog starter + the "architect beat" scheduled-task template.

  • Scaling the org: adding departments without adding chaos
  • Your 30/60/90 roadmap + where to take it next
